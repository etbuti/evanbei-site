Two-Track Rental Operation – Operating Standard

This page documents the agreed rental operating method for two properties. It is designed for clarity, stability, compliance, and long-term execution.

Operating Principle

Both properties may be listed, but it is acceptable that only one is actively let at a given time. The other remains flexible by design (premium positioning / selective letting).
Track A High-value • Self-managed • Selective Track B Standard rent • Agent-managed • Stable
Property A → Track A (premium, self-managed, selective letting) Property B → Track B (standard, fully managed by agent) Goal: stable operation without emotional decision-making. Switching is operational, not emotional.

Track A – High-Value Selective Letting

Self-managed

Objective: premium price through premium clarity. We select tenants proactively and reduce occupancy risk.

Structured Flow
Application → Screening → References → Agreement → Ongoing service
Tenant Selection (Non-discriminatory)
  • Professionals preferred; stable income and references required.
  • No subletting. No short-term letting. Named occupants only.
  • Clear house rules sent before signing.
  • Responsive maintenance: target response within 24–48 hours for typical issues.
House Rules (Summary)
  • Occupancy: no sharers, no subletting, named occupants only.
  • Use: residential only; no business or short-term letting.
  • Noise: respect neighbours; no parties/events.
  • Maintenance: report issues promptly; landlord responds within agreed timeframe.
  • Termination: break clause after 6 months (or agreed); notice per tenancy.
Track A quick checklist
  • Written application form collected
  • Right to Rent checks completed
  • Employment + previous landlord references
  • Deposit handled correctly by the chosen scheme
  • Inventory (photos + list) stored
  • House rules acknowledged in writing

Track B – Standard Managed Letting

Agent-managed

Objective: cash-flow stability with minimal landlord involvement. Standard service only.

Agent Instructions (Core)
  • Pricing: market rent; review at day 14 and day 28.
  • Fees: management fee charged only when rent is collected; no fee during void periods.
  • Repairs: agent authorised up to £150 without approval; above requires written consent.
  • Tenant Management: agent handles all tenant communication; landlord not involved in daily matters.
Minimum Tenant Standard
  • Right to Rent compliant
  • Referencing completed
  • No known rent arrears history
Track B void-control rules (0–45 days)
  • Day 14: if viewings are weak → adjust price/photos/listing quality.
  • Day 28: must move to “deal price” (market median) or offer incentive.
  • Day 45: review agent performance; switch agent if needed.

Switching Logic (Pre-defined)

Switching is operational, not emotional. We keep a flexible buffer by design.

Switch to Track A when

  • Higher control is required (tenant selection, price protection).
  • Flexible use is preferred (buffer position).
  • Market conditions justify premium pricing.

Switch to Track B when

  • Stability is prioritised (faster letting, less involvement).
  • Reduced day-to-day work is needed.
  • Market conditions favour standard letting.
Red lines (do not do)
  • Do not refuse viewings while claiming the property is “available”.
  • Do not make the premium listing “impossibly priced” for a long period without review.
  • Do not mix accounts/records between properties or owners.
  • Do not use discriminatory language in listings or selection.
