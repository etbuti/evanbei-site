Research Focus

Evan Bei is an independent scholar working at the intersection of sound studies, time-based practice, and conceptual research. His work approaches sound not as a fixed object or representational medium, but as an event shaped by temporal decision, embodiment, and contextual conditions.

Core Project

Sound as Event is a long-term research project that develops a conceptual framework for understanding sound as an occurrence rather than an object.

  • Breath as Event — embodied conditions of initiation (whether sound may occur)
  • Pulse as Event — temporal decision structures (when sound may occur)
  • Voice as Event — execution and presence (who/what performs the sound)

Short Bio (100 words)

Evan Bei is an independent scholar whose work explores sound as a temporally situated event rather than as a fixed object or representational medium. His research focuses on conditions of sound occurrence, including embodiment, temporal decision, and execution. His current project, Sound as Event, develops a conceptual framework through applied studies on breath, pulse, and voice, offering a non-representational approach to sound-based practice across experimental music, sound art, and interdisciplinary contexts.
