Research Focus

Evan Bei is an independent researcher working at the intersection of sound studies, time-based practice, and conceptual research. The work approaches sound not as a fixed object or representational medium, but as an event articulated through temporal decision, embodied conditions, and contextual relations.

Core Project

Sound as Event is a long-term research project developing a conceptual framework for understanding sound in event-based terms rather than as an object.

  • Breath as Event — embodied conditions of initiation (the conditions under which sound may occur)
  • Pulse as Event — temporal decision structures (the temporal logic by which sound may occur)
  • Voice as Event — execution and presence (the agencies through which sound occurs)

Short Bio

Evan Bei is an independent researcher whose work examines sound as a temporally situated event. The research focuses on embodiment, temporal decision, and execution as conditions through which sound emerges. The ongoing project Sound as Event develops a conceptual framework through studies on breath, pulse, and voice, proposing a non-representational approach to sound-based practice across experimental music, sound art, and interdisciplinary contexts.
